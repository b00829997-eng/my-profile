What is my dream?

My dream is for every talented and motivated student to build an academic and professional path truly aligned with their strengths . I aim to guide them through their first steps at university with practical tools, critical reflections, and authentic experiences, making knowledge accessible and promoting a culture rooted in merit, conscious commitment, and continuous growth.

I aspire to create an environment where everyone can express their full potential with confidence, dedication, and intellectual curiosity, putting shared experience at the service of everyone's future.

What is my purpose?

My mission is to accompany other students in their first steps at university , offering practical tools, critical reflections, and effective strategies to approach studies , career choices, and personal growth with awareness. Through quality educational content and sharing my direct experiences, I want to make knowledge more accessible and promote merit as a lever for developing one's potential.

My goal is to spread a culture of intelligent commitment, authenticity, and responsibility, helping those who follow me to discover their strengths and build a path that is consistent and satisfying with their aspirations .

What do I believe in?

My Values are:

  • Merit and dedication : success is born from consistent effort and a genuine passion for knowledge
  • Sharing : personal experience gains real value only when it is put at the service of others
  • Continuous growth : learning is not a destination, but a lifelong process
  • Accessibility of knowledge : every student, regardless of their starting point, should have the opportunity to express their full potential
  • Authenticity : lead by example, communicating with honesty, rigor, and intellectual curiosity

What have I done so far?

My name is Italo,

I was born in Bologna, and I grew up in a house full of books a place where we talked about everything: philosophy, politics, science, economics . That's probably where my curiosity came from the desire to understand how ideas turn into actions, and how learning can truly change people.

After high school, I enrolled at Bocconi University, where I studied Economics and Social Sciences. At first, it was tough: new rhythms, strong competition, demanding exams. But that's where I learned the value of discipline: waking up early, studying consistently, organizing my time, and never stopping asking questions. I graduated with highest honors, then continued with a master's in Philosophy and Public Policy at Oxford, where I discovered how powerful it can be to connect critical thinking with the concreteness of economics.

Today, I want to share what I've learned with students who are just starting out those who want to give their best, but also live their university journey with awareness and curiosity. Through my videos, I share methods, experiences, and reflections to help others build a future that matches their effort and their potential.
